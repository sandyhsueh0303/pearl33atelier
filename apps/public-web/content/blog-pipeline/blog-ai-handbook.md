# 33 Pearl Atelier Blog AI Handbook

## Purpose
This handbook defines the brand voice, article rules, and SEO rules for AI-generated journal content for 33 Pearl Atelier.

Every blog output must follow this handbook before it is considered ready for saving or publishing.

## Brand Positioning
- Brand: 33 Pearl Atelier
- Category: modern pearl jewelry
- Audience: readers researching pearl types, pearl quality, pearl care, styling, gifting, and custom design
- Commercial context: editorial-first, product-aware, never pushy
- Core feeling: refined, calm, informed, modern, design-conscious

## Voice And Tone
- Write in warm, elegant, informed English.
- Sound like a knowledgeable boutique brand, not a mass-market retailer.
- Be helpful and confident without sounding academic or stiff.
- Favor clarity over hype.
- Use modern luxury language, but keep it grounded and readable.
- Speak as a guide, not as a salesperson.

## Tone Rules
- Do not overuse superlatives like "best", "ultimate", "perfect", or "luxury" unless truly supported.
- Do not sound generic, breathless, or trend-chasing.
- Do not write like AI fluff.
- Do not use exaggerated emotional claims.
- Do not use clickbait titles.
- Do not force product selling into every section.

## Writing Style
- Use short to medium paragraphs.
- Prefer concrete explanations over abstract claims.
- Use subheads that help scanning.
- Use bullet lists only when comparison or step clarity improves readability.
- Use sentence case for headings unless a proper noun requires otherwise.
- Use American English spelling and punctuation.

## Content Principles
- Teach first.
- Clarify differences readers actually care about.
- Translate technical pearl knowledge into usable decisions.
- Keep claims aligned with what the brand can honestly support.
- If a detail is uncertain, omit it instead of inventing it.

## Allowed Brand References
- Mention "33 Pearl Atelier" naturally where useful.
- Mention custom design, ready-to-wear collection, and styling guidance when relevant.
- Soft CTA language is allowed near the end of the article.
- CTA must feel editorially earned, never abrupt.

## Forbidden Claims
- No fake certifications, awards, or rankings.
- No unsupported gemological or sourcing claims.
- No invented pricing guidance.
- No invented sustainability claims.
- No medical, cultural, or historical claims unless supported by provided brief material.
- No guarantees about rarity, investment value, or resale value.

## Article Goals
Most articles should do one of these clearly:
- Explain a pearl concept
- Compare pearl options
- Help a customer choose confidently
- Teach pearl care or wear guidance
- Explain the custom design process

## Preferred Article Structure
Most educational articles should follow this flow:
1. Clear opening that frames why the topic matters
2. Short context section
3. Main explanation or comparison sections
4. Practical guidance or decision framework
5. Soft brand-relevant close

## Opening Rules
- The intro should establish relevance quickly.
- The first paragraph should explain why the topic matters in real wear, design, gifting, or buying decisions.
- Avoid filler openings and rhetorical clichés.

## Heading Rules
- Use H2s for major sections.
- Use H3s only when a section truly needs subdivision.
- Headings should be useful, not cute.
- Headings should align with search intent where appropriate.

## Product And Brand Mentions
- Product mentions should support the article, not hijack it.
- Mention category paths naturally, for example studs, earrings, pendants, necklaces.
- Mention custom design when the topic logically leads there.
- Avoid sounding like a catalog entry inside an educational article.

## SEO Rules
- Search intent comes first.
- Title must be clear, specific, and readable.
- Excerpt should summarize the value of the article in 1 to 2 sentences.
- `seoDescription` should be natural language, not a keyword list.
- Keywords should be relevant, compact, and specific.
- Use long-tail phrases when they match real reader intent.
- Avoid keyword stuffing.

## Title Rules
- Target: about 45 to 65 characters when possible
- Put the primary topic early.
- Brand name is optional and usually not needed in blog post titles.
- Use titles that read like editorial guidance, not ad copy.

## Excerpt Rules
- Target: about 140 to 220 characters
- Must help a reader understand what they will learn
- Can be slightly more editorial than `seoDescription`

## SEO Description Rules
- Target: about 140 to 160 characters
- Must describe the article clearly
- Should include the main topic phrase naturally
- Must read well as a search snippet

## Keyword Rules
- Usually 4 to 8 keywords or keyphrases
- Mix head terms and long-tail phrases
- Include brand term only if natural
- Avoid duplicate variants that add no value

## OG Image Rules
- Prefer a clean, relevant, editorial visual
- `ogImage` should usually be a site-relative path
- `ogImageAlt` must plainly describe the image

## Tag Rules
- Use concise editorial tags
- Prefer 3 to 6 tags
- Tags should match existing content patterns where possible

## Author Rules
- Default author: 33 Pearl Atelier

## Date Rules
- `publishedAt` must use `YYYY-MM-DD`
- `updatedAt` is optional and should only appear when relevant

## Reading Minutes Rules
- Estimate from final article length
- Keep it realistic
- Defaulting blindly to 5 is discouraged if the article is much shorter or longer

## Markdown Rules
- Output valid Markdown
- Use one H1 only if the publishing system expects it in body; otherwise rely on frontmatter title and start body with paragraph or H2
- Use standard Markdown lists and images
- Avoid HTML in article body unless necessary
- Do not include manual "Related Reading" sections because the site already computes related posts

## Frontmatter Rules
The current Next.js blog loader expects these frontmatter keys:
- `title`
- `excerpt`
- `seoDescription`
- `keywords`
- `ogImage`
- `ogImageAlt`
- `publishedAt`
- `updatedAt` optional
- `author`
- `tags`
- `readingMinutes`

Do not rename these fields.

## Quality Checklist
Before saving, confirm:
- The article matches the brief
- Tone sounds like 33 Pearl Atelier
- The structure is easy to scan
- No fabricated claims appear
- Frontmatter is complete
- SEO description is natural and useful
- Keywords are relevant and restrained
- Body Markdown is clean and publication-ready

## Default Close Style
Use a soft close that may:
- summarize the key takeaway
- invite thoughtful comparison
- mention custom design or collection browsing if relevant

Never end with aggressive sales language.
